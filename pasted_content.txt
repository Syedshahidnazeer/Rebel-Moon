You’re building a card-drawing (gacha) site where each pull reveals an AI-generated mythical scene tied to the Taj Mahal and Indo‑Islamic motifs: celestial gardens, moonlit domes, calligraphic guardians, river spirits along the Yamuna, etc.

The tone is Indian Muslim luxury: think white marble, inlaid gemstones, gold foil, geometric arabesques, moonlight, and soft recitation‑like ambience, but balanced with a minimalist layout and plenty of breathing room.

3. Visual system (Taj Mahal × minimalist fantasy)
Colors (roles, not exact hexes)
Background:

Deep midnight navy or blue‑black (like a night sky above the Taj), so white marble elements glow.

Primary surface:

Soft ivory/white marble with a faint stone texture on cards and panels, hinting at the Taj’s marble facades.

Primary accent:

Gold (muted, not neon) for lines, borders, rarity tags, and calligraphic motifs.

Secondary accents:

Emerald and sapphire for rare and mythic frames (echoing gem inlays).

Typography colors:

Main text: warm light beige for comfort against dark blue.

Muted text: slightly dimmer beige for secondary labels.

Typography
Headings:

Elegant serif with slight calligraphic vibe (think display serif with high contrast).

Use for H1/H2, large titles on cards.

Body:

Clean sans‑serif for legibility on mobile.

Lore text:

Slightly more line height, maybe small caps or italic for headings like “Lore”.

Accent details:

Occasional decorative ligature or stylized ampersand in headers.

Layout & grid
Mobile-first:

Single column. Hero, then gacha panel, then current card, then gallery, etc.

Cards occupy ~90% width with generous padding and rounded corners.

Tablet/desktop:

2-column layout in the main interaction area (card and controls side by side).

Gallery as 3–4 column grid.

Spacing:

Large vertical spacing between sections to preserve the “museum” feel, not a cramped game UI.

Recurring motifs
Mughal arches and frames:

Use a subtle arch shape on top of the card art area, echoing Taj doorways.

Geometric borders:

Thin gold geometric frames around cards, inspired by Islamic ornamental borders.

Soft glow halos:

Behind rare cards, a faint circular gradient to feel blessed or celestial.

Crescent / moon icon:

Used as a subtle loading spinner or rarity indicator.

4. Interaction & motion design
Gacha draw flow
Trigger: Tap “Draw a card”.

Animation sequence:

Deck on pedestal trembles slightly, then one card rises.

Card floats upward (translateY, scale up), then rotates 180° (Y-axis) to reveal the face.

Background behind card dims slightly, adding spotlight.

Lore text fades in separately (small delay) and rarity badge appears with a flicker.

Duration & easing:

300–400ms lifts, 250ms flip, 200ms fade for text, using ease-out / cubic-bezier for smoothness.

Purpose:

Make each draw feel significant without long delays. Cards are “ceremonial objects”, not slot‑machine reels.

Bestiary/gallery interactions
Card hover/tap:

On hover (desktop): subtle tilt and parallax of art area.

On tap (mobile): scale up slightly and open detail view in a full-screen overlay with slide-up animation.

Filters:

Chips (All / Rare / Mythic) animate with underlines and micro-scale when selected.

Filter action smoothly re‑orders or fades cards rather than hard cuts.

Global motion
Scroll:

Section titles use a subtle fade‑up on entering viewport.

Background stars or light dust can have a very slow parallax or drift.

prefers-reduced-motion:

Disable flips and parallax, replace with simple fade-in of card faces.
